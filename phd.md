My PhD thesis is about modeling and control of a flexible-fluid coupled system. What?!

At [ISAE](www.isae.fr), we have an experimental set up that consists of an aluminium plate with
a (water) tank near the tip. The idea is that this device has some similarity to an airplane wing with
a tip tank. The structure and the fluid both moves at similar speeds, and their dynamics are coupled
as you can see in the following video:

![First mode video]({{ site.baseurl }}images/video1.gif)

So my thesis has two main goals: 1) find appropriate mathematical models for this experiment (modeling);
2) find appropriated methods to reduce the vibrations (control). For controlling the system, we are using
[piezoelectric](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piezoelectricity) patches (which consists in a kind of material that
deforms when a voltage is applied).

Some partial results of my study were presented in the following papers: 

* [Modeling of a coupled fluid-structure system excited by piezoelectric actuators](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/263927713_Modeling_of_a_Coupled_Fluid-Structure_System_Excited_by_Piezoelectric_Actuators):
presented at IEEE/ASME AIM 2014;
* [Control design for a coupled fluid-structure system with piezoelectric actuators](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/274896289_Control_design_for_a_coupled_fluid-structure_system_with_piezoelectric_actuators),
presented at EuroGNC 2015.

Since 2014, I've been working with port-Hamiltonian approach for modeling and control
of flexible-fluid structures.
An introduction about this subject as well as first results are presented [here]({{ site.baseurl }}port_hamiltonian).
